# User Guide

## First Launch

The first time you open the app, you'll be asked to enter your name. This is
only used to identify you to opponents in Nearby Multiplayer mode, and is
saved on the device so you won't be asked again.

## Choosing a Mode

After the name entry (or immediately, on later launches), you'll see two
options:

- **Same Device** — for two people sharing one phone
- **Nearby Multiplayer** — for two separate phones, connected directly
  without internet

## Playing Same Device

- Player 1 is **X**, Player 2 is **O**. Tap any empty cell to place your mark.
- The winning line highlights when someone wins.
- Tap **New Game** (shown after a win/draw) or **Reset Game** (always
  available) to start over. Whoever went second in the last game goes first
  in the next one, so both players get a fair share of first moves over
  multiple rounds.

## Playing Nearby Multiplayer

1. From the mode select screen, tap **Nearby Multiplayer**.
2. One phone should tap **Host Game**, the other **Join Game**.
3. **On first use**, Android will ask for a few permissions — allow all of
   them, or the connection won't work:
   - **Bluetooth** (scan, advertise, connect) — used to find and connect to
     the other phone
   - **Nearby devices / WiFi** — used for the actual data connection
   - **Location** — Android requires this for Bluetooth/WiFi scanning; the
     app does not use your location for anything else
4. On the joining phone, wait for the host to appear in the list, then tap it
   to connect.
5. Once connected, the host plays as **X** and moves first; the joining
   player plays as **O**.
6. Play as normal — moves appear on both phones instantly.
7. Either player can tap **New Game** or **Reset Game** to start a new round.
   Who goes first alternates each round automatically, and stays in sync
   between both phones no matter who tapped the button.

## Troubleshooting

- **Host not showing up on the joining phone**: make sure both phones have
  Bluetooth and WiFi turned on, are within a few meters of each other, and
  that you accepted all permission prompts.
- **"Connection failed"**: back out to the lobby and try hosting/joining
  again — nearby connections can occasionally need a retry.
- **Opponent disconnected mid-game**: this is shown on screen if the other
  phone loses connection (e.g. moved out of range, backgrounded the app).
